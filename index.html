BOQ gaps

Several preliminaries items are present in the pricing basis but do not appear clearly carried through the measured sections. Confirm whether temporary works, testing, and traffic accommodation are fully included.

Inconsistent rates

Concrete-related rates vary materially across similar work items without an obvious explanation. Recheck whether the labour, plant, and material build-up is still aligned across the tender.

Assumptions / exclusions wording

The exclusions are directionally useful but still leave room for dispute. Tighten language on supply responsibility, lead times, access constraints, and weekend working.

Submission-readiness note

Commercially close, but not yet clean. Resolve the flagged scope items, confirm risk allowances, and reissue before submission to protect margin and reduce post-award friction.

FAQ

Short answers for the questions contractors usually ask first.

Who is ClearTender for?

ClearTender is for contractors across the market, with strongest emphasis on building and civil contractors who need a proper commercial second review before pricing goes out.

Is this engineering signoff or QS certification?

No. ClearTender reviews pricing and commercial structure. It does not replace engineering signoff, QS certification, or legal advice.

What do you need from me?

Ideally the BOQ, drawings, pricing basis, addenda, and any exclusions or assumptions already drafted by your team.

Can you work from incomplete information?

Yes, but the review is stronger when the pack is complete. Gaps will be flagged as risks rather than guessed away.
